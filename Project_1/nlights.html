The Northern Lights



THE OTHERWORLDLY AURORA borealis, or northern lights, begin high in the Earths atmosphere—at altitudes from 60 to more than 250 miles— when charged particles from the sun become trapped in the Earth's magnetic field. The result is a colorful, dancing light show.
